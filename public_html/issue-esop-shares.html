Issue ESOP/SWEAT Equity Shares

Issuance of sweat equity shares or formulation and implementation of Employee Stock Option Plan or Scheme “ESOP” or “ESOS” by Unlisted Companies (public or private) is primarily governed by the relevant provisions of Companies Act, 1956 ”Act” and Unlisted Companies (Issue of Sweat Equity Shares) Rules, 2003 “Rules” issued under Companies Act, 1956. If the total shareholders after allotment of share in the ESOP scheme reach 200 or more then the company shall convert itself to the public limited company.

FAQs

  • What is the difference between sweat equity and ESOP?

    The difference between sweat Sweat Equity and ESOP are as follows: Sweat Equity is grant of shares at discount or without monetary considerations whereas Employee Stock Option Plan (ESOP) / Employee Stock Option Scheme Scheme (ESOS) is grant of option to purchase share at predetermined price given to employees.

  • What is sweat equity shares?

    Sweat equity shares means such equity shares as are issued by a company to its directors or employees at a discount or for consideration, other than cash, for providing their know-how or making available rights in the nature of intellectual property rights or value additions, by whatever name called.

  • Can sweat equity shares be issued to promoters?

    Yes, Sweat equity shares can be issued to promoters but, the limitation is that, before issuing, company has to be adjourned a general meeting and will get a simple majority of Shareholders about promoters.
